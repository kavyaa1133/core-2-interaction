Would you like to share a past story or an experience navigating through the city?

Positive Stories

“I was lost in the city late at night, and a woman noticed. She walked me all the way back to my apartment. It was such a small thing, but it made me feel safe in a way I hadn’t expected.”
“I was walking home in the rain without an umbrella, and a man stopped to offer me his. It wasn’t just the umbrella—it was the kindness that came with it.”
“I was on the subway, and a woman saw that I was pregnant and offered me her seat. That small gesture made me feel seen.”
“I was feeling down one day, and a stranger smiled at me. It was such an unexpected, kind gesture, and it felt good to know that there is calmness around me.”
“One night, I felt really unsafe walking home. But a woman walked some of the way with me. It felt like the city was reminding me that I was part of a bigger, supportive community.”
“A bus driver waited an extra minute when they saw me running toward the stop. It wasn’t a big deal to them, but it made my whole day.”

Negative Stories

“I was walking down a street near my apartment and a man started following me. It wasn’t until I made a sharp turn into a busy café that he stopped, but that moment felt like the world was closing in on me.”
“I was at a bar with some friends, and a guy came up and wouldn’t leave me alone despite me clearly saying I wasn’t interested. It was uncomfortable, and I had to step away to get some space.”
“I was on the subway and some guy kept staring at me, making me feel super uncomfortable. I tried to ignore him, but the whole ride was tense and anxiety-inducing. I just wanted to get off.”
“Once, I was in a crowded area and a man brushed up against me in a way that felt intentional. I quickly moved away, but it left me feeling violated, even though it wasn’t overtly aggressive.”
“Late one night, I was walking home and a group of men started yelling things at me. I tried to walk faster, but it made me feel like I was being hunted.”
“My friend and I were at a halal truck when a stranger approached, asking us to dinner and starting a weird conversation. We tried to ignore him, but we felt so uncomfortable.”
